Management of Mechanical Lifting Mechanical Lifting Checklist

You must be trained in Mechanical Lifting Modules 1-4 to complete this checklist

a. For all lifts from wells - criteria for single person lift:

Do you know if the load is 200kg or less?
Is the lift in daylight or adequate fixed lighting?
Have you worked less than a 12 hour shift?

If No to any of the above you must request a 2nd person

b. For lifts from wells and chambers:

If there is a need to remove a section of grid flooring will all the remaining section be secured in position i.e. clamped or cable tied?

If the answer to the above question is No, STOP work and secure the sections with clamps or steel cable ties

Are fall protection arrangements adequate for this lift? State below which method is in place/being used
I. Permanent fixed barriers
II. Fixed cover with integral gated grid
III.Quatrosafe barrier, Quatrosafe large pump lifting grid or AW's pump lifting grid - can only be used with minimum of 2 people present

I,II or III must be yes. If not, STOP WORK-there MUST be fall protection arrangements in place and the safe system of work MUST be detailed in a Site Specific Task Risk Assessment. Temporary scaffolding or barriers may be required, they must be robust and stable to prevent someone falling into the well/chamber

c. For all lifts - preparing for the lift:

Is all lifting equipment suitably marked ("Scafftagged" etc.) and in date?
Is appropriate PPE being worn by all parties involved i.e. hard hat, gloves, overalls, safety footwear, high visibility jackets.
Is the lifting equipment adequate for the anticipated load?
Have pre-use checks been carried out on the lifting equipment?
Has the load been isolated from electrical / mechanical attachment?
Can the load be manoeuvred to a safe position on site?

If the answer to any of the above questions is No, STOP work and seek management guidance

d. For all lifts - planning for the lift:

Have you considered the shape, dimension and balance of the load?
Have the weather conditions been taken into account?
Does suitable lighting exist, either natural or artificial?
Are ground conditions and working area suitable for the lift?
Confident that no underground or overhead cables/plant poses a hazard?
Can the lifting equipment be erected or positioned safely?
Can the load be slewed safely?
Does the lifting area have a suitable cordon?

If the answer to any of the above questions is No, STOP work - a site specific task risk assessment MUST be completed to determine if the lift can be carried out safely

e. For lifts where breakout forces exist:

I. Can a load cell or slipping clutch be used throughout the lift?
II. If not, is there an adequate safety factor of at least 2:1?

I or II must be yes. If not, STOP WORK, - a site specific task risk assessment MUST be completed

f. For single person lifts:

With all of the relevant controls above in place - can you safely carry out a single person lift?
