Live Diagnostic Testing

To be used for the testing of exposed (not IP2X) live low voltage (up to 1000 volts AC or up to 1500 volts DC) equipment for the purpose of determining its condition, performance or operation (including the testing of supply synchronisation units - G59 testing).

Battery and electrical systems which do not exceed 55 volts, for example, vehicle, mobile plant and remote monitoring systems are exempt from the need for live diagnostic testing checklist.

1. It should only be used as a last resort for fault finding.
2. Live diagnostic checklist is not required for circuits specifically designed for safe testing, i.e. control panels with safety test switches, circuits not exceeding 55 volts, or where all electrical equipment is shielded to at least the IP2X standard.
3. It can only be carried out by category 1, 2 or 3 electricians.
4. The Live Diagnostic Testing checklist shall be fully completed before any live diagnostic testing takes place.
5. Please remember that the second person is there for safety purposes only and should not be involved in the work. This person does not necessarily need to be an electrician but must be over 18 years of age, and able to:
1. ensure that others (3rd parties) are kept at a safe distance.
2. raise the alarm, if there is a problem
isolate the electrical supply (if safe to do so) in an emergency
3.administer emergency first aid if required
