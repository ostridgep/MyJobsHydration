Guidance on the completion of the Classification Risk Assessment

1. Do not use this form unless you have been trained. Training will be given either by your line manager and/or during your formal re-training / assessment

2. This form should be used to assess and classify low risk (NC1) confined spacesat least every three years. Manager shall keep records for at least six years.

3. For all confined spaces other than low risk (NC1), the form shall be completed prior to each entry to determine the risk level and therefore the controls required for safe working

4. If carrying out a number of entries into the same confined space over one day and conditions have not changed between each entry then it is only necessary to complete one form. If the job runs into a second day however, a new form must be completed.

5. Before attempting to calculate a score (to determine National Classification) it is very important that you first consider whether you can either eliminate going into the confined space or reduce the risk of harm to as low as is reasonably practicable. Completion of the table at the top of the form will helpyou with this. In many cases this will result in lowering the risk in the confinedspace and make the job easier and safer for yourself and other team members.

6. Completion of the scoring system is straightforward - simply assess the chances of a hazardous atmosphere and access/egress into the confined spaceand multiply the two numbers together to get the total score.

7. Remember, when assessing the chance of a hazardous atmosphere you need to consider the likelihood of it causing harm. For example, small amounts of hydrogen sulphide which quickly dilutes will not cause harm but a hazardous gas throughout the working area will be a problem. In a similar way, working in a very shallow sewer manhole you may encounter some hazardous gas but you can quickly get yourself out of the hazard area by simply standing up in most cases, so again there is not a likely risk it will cause you harm.

8. Use the total score to assign a risk classification using the guidance table on the form. Carry out the work using the controls for that risk classification.

9. For NC4 entries please ensure that a member of the safety team has been contacted prior to the permit being issued.

10. Completed forms should be retained by the originator for at least three monthsfor audit purposes. Should an accident occur relating to the confined space task, the completed form must be passed to the line manager for retention.

11. If in any doubt contact your manager.
